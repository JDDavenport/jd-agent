The Right Set of Strategy Questions

THE RIGHT SET OF STRATEGY QUESTIONS (Based on the 4 Questions Framework):

  • WHERE DO WE COMPETE?
    1. What market(s) do we compete in?
    2. What are the boundaries of our industry/market?
    3. Who are the relevant players?
    4. What jobs are customers hiring this industry/market to do?
    5. How attractive is this environment?
      1. How attractive is this environment today?
        • What is the nature of competition in this market?
          • If this is a win-lose scenario, what moves are my smart competitors most likely to make? How can I anticipate and prepare for those moves?
          • If this is a win-win scenario, how can I make moves that enhance the success of all players?
          • Is competition profit enhancing (differentiation based) or profit destroying (price based)?
          • What are the strategies of the main competitors and how do/will their strategies affect us??
        • What impact do suppliers have on the profitability of this environment?
        • What impact do buyers have on the profitability of this environment?
        • What impact do substitutes have on the profitability of this environment?
        • What impact do new entrants have on the profitability of this environment?
      2. How attractive will this environment be tomorrow?
        • What trends are coming in the broader environment that may change the assessment of the attractiveness of the market?
        • What does the PEST analysis tell us about the future?
        • How is the job-to-be-done changing over time?
      3. Should we be competing somewhere else too?
        1. Should we forward or backward integrate?
          • Will a move enhance our capabilities, coordination and/or control?
          • Will the loss of flexibility or focus be acceptable?
        2. Should we horizontally diversify?
          • Can our resources be leveraged in a horizontal industry/market/environment?
          • Can we allocate capital more efficiently than the free market?

 

  • WHAT UNIQUE VALUE DO WE OFFER TO CUSTOMERS IN THIS ENVIRONMENT?
    1. Why do customers buy our product or service over all other products and services available to them? (price, value, service, all, none?)
    2. What is unique about the value we offer?

 

  • WHAT RESOURCES AND CAPABILITIES ALLOW US TO CONTINUE TO OFFER THAT VALUE?
    1. What do we have that our rivals do not?
    2. What resources do we have that are better than our rivals’ resources?
    3. What can we do better than our rivals?
    4. Are we organized to deliver that value?
      1. Is our organizational structure appropriate?
      2. Does our culture support the right employee behaviors and actions?
  • Do our incentive and reward systems align with the right organizational outcomes?
  1. Do our resources and capabilities support our strategy?
  2. Do our resources and capabilities fit with the current and future external environment?
  3. Are we organized to capture the value we create in the marketplace?

 

  • CAN WE SUSTAIN OUR ABILITY TO OFFER THAT UNIQUE VALUE TO CUSTOMERS?
    1. Are our resources and capabilities easy to imitate, replicate or transfer?
    2. Are we the only ones who can capture the value from our resources and capabilities?
