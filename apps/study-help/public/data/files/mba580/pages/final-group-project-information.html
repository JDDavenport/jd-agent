Final Group Project Information

MBA 580 Team Strategy Project Assignment

Dr. Gubler

Winter 2026

 

Purpose:

The purpose of the final team strategy project is to give your team an opportunity to work your way collaboratively through the strategic concepts, frameworks, tools, and theories presented in class, and to use them to help an organization identify and solve a strategic problem. This experience gives you additional practice describing the company’s strategy, doing strategic analysis, diagnosing problems, developing a strategic recommendation, and thinking about challenges they may encounter deploying your recommendation (these are the 4 Ds of strategy found on www.learnstrategy.org and aligns with the strategy rejuvenation process—finding the crux—we discussed in class). It also helps us attain our class learning objectives, which include applying the content to real world situations, working in teams, improving our ability to communicate and “sell” our ideas, and giving back through Christlike service.  

Audience:

Approach this project as a team of strategy consultants hired to 1) perform a strategic audit on a company, 2) identify a pressing strategic problem or opportunity, and 3) provide a concrete and actionable recommendation to resolve the strategic problem or to seize the strategic opportunity. Because you will present your project to the class (outsiders), as well as to your company (insiders), you will have to take a unique approach. For the presentation to the company, you should plan to share an amended version of the class presentations. However, for the presentations in class you will have to assume that you are presenting your work to a board of directors that has just been revamped, where several of the board members are brand new with limited prior knowledge of the business (this audience is me and your classmates). Thus, you need to provide a brief but complete introduction to the business, its strategy, and the industry.

Choosing a Company:

You are free to choose a company as a team to focus on and to use any data available to you. In the past I have seen many MBA teams add significant value to small and medium sized local companies through these projects. Picking the “right” company to work with is critical to having a positive experience, and to delivering a quality project. Thus, there are a few guidelines and rules we will use when considering companies. First, the rules. You must choose a company with at least 5 employees, to make sure the company is large enough to warrant focus from your team, and to lead to a strong learning experience. Second, the company needs to be generating revenues (we want to avoid start-ups or any pre-revenue company). Pre-revenue companies are difficult to work with for this final project, and often do not provide a great learning experience. Third, you must ensure at the start of the project that you have the necessary access to company data (including financial/performance data) and key individuals to justify focusing your time on this company. This should be spelled out in the statement of work and communicated with the company contact. Finally, it should be a company that the entire group has interest in studying, and not a family company with which one of the teammates has a strong familial connection. Next, the guidelines. When approaching your company you should explain that there are two parts to this project, and they need to be committed to work with you throughout the process. First, you are required to do a strategic audit of their company, where you apply the key frameworks and tools to understand their strategy and to diagnose a strategic problem or opportunity. After this strategic audit is completed, you will present your results to the company (approximately mid-way through the semester). Second, after the strategic audit, you will choose a problem to focus on together (the crux!) that aligns with the results of the strategic audit and that is of value to the company. Ultimately, your goal is to provide value both through the strategic audit as well as in the recommendation you ultimately provide.

Before you proceed with your project, you will need to get approval from Professor Gubler. Any exceptions to the rules above need to be cleared by Professor Gubler. A statement of work with your company choice and the intended plan and deliverables for the project will be due on (February 17th, 2026). This is helpful to collaborate on and share with your company, although you do NOT need it to be signed by your the company. If you want company approval before February 17th please send me an email. In the email explain the company you have chosen and whether you will have good access to the company and data, to allow you to complete the strategy audit. If you need to sign an NDA to work with your company, please discuss this with me as a team so we can review best practices and ensure this turns into a good learning experience for you and the company. The company must understand that engaging in a project may result in some educational exposure of company information and data. It is not uncommon for students to sign NDAs when working on these projects.

Deliverables:

The first deliverable for this project is a team PowerPoint presentation in class on March 3rd. In this presentation you should introduce your chosen company, describe its strategy (FOCUS framework + accompanying frameworks that help to understand the operating environment, competition, unique value proposition, value creation system, and moats), and explain the strategic problem or opportunity you see that needs to be resolved (i.e., the crux). You will present in class, and the time limit will be approximately 12 minutes. 

The second deliverable for this project is a final team PowerPoint presentation in class on April 7-14. This presentation will be to the entire class, and the order of presenting is noted online under the schedule on Canvas. The time limit for this presentation will be 14 minutes, after which we will have a few minutes for class Q&A. This presentation should focus on quickly describing the company and strategy, articulating the crux, and presenting your recommendation. The final project slide deck will be due before the start of the FIRST presentations, on April 7th. This levels the playing field across groups, regardless of the time slot they receive to present.

The third deliverable is a memo that confirms the presentation of your project to your company mid-way through he semester, and also at the end (two times). You are required to deliver the results of your project to someone in your company. This is due (one memo) April 18th at midnight.

Finally, you will individually provide feedback and a grade on the presentations you view in class for the two presentation. This feedback will help each team improve their project, or to reflect on how they have done (remember the principles of critical thinking we discussed!) and will provide an opportunity for you to carefully think through how other teams approached a difficult problem. This assignment will be posted on Canvas.  

To support the final project, you will also submit a team contract near the beginning of the semester, (due February 10th) which details how you will work as a team, as well as a statement of work (due February 17th). This statement of work functions as a contract of sorts between your team and the company. 

Content:

For the final project, you should draw on any frameworks, tools, theories, or concepts needed to audit the firm, identify the strategic problem, and to provide a value add recommendation/solution. As this is a group assignment, also draw on the experience, expertise, and skills of each team-member and work together to think critically and creatively to generate recommendations. The “divide and conquer” method to working in a team should be minimized in this type of project to ensure a good learning experience and a high-quality final project.

There are a few required elements. This includes:

  • Clearly describing and auditing your company’s current strategy to understand how they are performing relative to competitors (focus more in first presentation). Strong work requires you to do quantitative analyses and to collect and analyze data. 
  • Performing required analyses to formulate and understand the crux (focus for both presentations)
  • Developing specific and actionable recommendations for how they should proceed (focus on in the second presentation). Depth is more important than breadth here. It should be clear that the recommendations are feasible and how they will resolve the crux. 
  • Collecting primary data to support the above pieces (from focus groups, interviews, surveys, the company, etc.). Of course you will also need to collect and analyze useful secondary data, and some of this will likely be unavailable to the company. (usually shows up more in the second presentation)

All strategy tools/frameworks/perspectives are important, but some may be more important than others given your particular company and its situation. They may also function as the base on which you form more custom analyses (particularly as you work on the second/final presentation). Thus, you will need to make decisions as a team about what is most important and convey your working knowledge of strategy through the decisions you make. I may ask in the Q&A after your presentation about strategy tools/frameworks/perspectives that you do/do not discuss, and my expectation is that you will be able to explain your why you chose to use (or not) that tool/framework/perspective. 

Grading:

You will be graded on three elements. The first will be the quality of your team presentations. While I will consider the feedback provided by class members after your team presentation, ultimately my grade is the only grade that matters. This grade will be provided to everyone on your team. Second, you will be graded as a group based on the quality of your submitted slide decks. This deck should show exactly what you have done, and be a strong product themselves. Including notes for slides and additional appendix slides can be helpful here in addition to effective slide design. Finally, you will be evaluated by your teammates (confidentially) to assess the extent to which you have pulled “equal weight” on the project throughout the semester and adhered to the team contract. If you carry less than an equal share, your final grade will be docked accordingly.

Content and presentation quality will factor into your grade. Thus, your final grade will reflect not only the quality of your analyses and recommendations, but also how well you present them in class and in your final report. This is a key element of business—being able to present and sell your ideas well. A rubric will be posted on Canvas that shows how I will assess your presentations and final slide decks. However, the rubric will relate to these key questions:

Evaluation Key Questions:

1. Has the team clearly and compellingly described the business strategy?

    1. Are the key strategy tools and frameworks represented and utilized appropriately?
    2. Is it clear what the business does and why it works?
    3. Has the team shown evidence of collecting and analyzing needed data?

2. Has the team clearly and compellingly diagnosed the problem or opportunity (crux)?

        1. Has the team used relevant strategy tools or frameworks to give insight into the crux?
        2. Do the key insights from the diagnosis come out clearly and convincingly in the presentation? Is the crux strongly supported by data and analyses?
        3. Has the team shown evidence of collecting and analyzing needed data?

3. Has the team developed compelling and actionable recommendations (major or minor adjustments to strategy)?

          1. Are the recommendations for the new strategy feasible and reasonable?
          2. Do the recommendations clearly fit with the problem/opportunity the team identified in their analysis?
          3. Is it clear that the recommendations fit the company’s strategy?
          4. Are the recommendations developed and justified enough that the company knows how to proceed? Can they do something because of the recommendations provided?

4. Has the team presented their project in a convincing and compelling way? This includes the in-class presentation as well as in the accompanying slide deck. Note that appendix slides can be included with additional data/analyses. Slides should be insight right and professional. 

Coaching and Support:

To help you succeed on the final project, I highly recommend that you use myself and the TAs to the fullest extent possible. I would love to meet with every team at least once to discuss your analyses and findings, and I hope to aid you along the way by providing effective coaching and feedback. However, it is largely up to your team to schedule and manage these interactions in a way that benefits your team. If you find yourself struggling with group dynamics, I can provide some tips, and aid as a moderator using the team contract. On the team work days, I will provide open blocks for you to sign up to meet with me as a team to discuss your progress and to receive coaching.

While the TAs and I are here to support you, the final project should ultimately include your own ideas and own work. Take ownership over what you are doing and stand behind your ideas. Create value and embrace the journey!

Ultimately, this project is provided to allow us to achieve the learning objectives of the class and to allow for hands-on experiential learning that will benefit you in getting a job and adding value as an employee. Thus, I encourage you to "lean in" and make this a meaningful project instead of the opposite. It is typically possible to conduct analyses on your final project company during the majority of the semester as an academic exercise. However, once you are actively developing work product for the company this may require you to use academic training time. In previous years teams with international students have sometimes chosen to work with BYU directly for their project (e.g., the BYU flower shop, the BookStore, etc.), to minimize use of academic training time. This is one option, although ultimately it is up to the group and my recommendation is to engage in as meaningful a project as possible. I am available to discuss these issues with you. The Global Management Center and the BYU international student office are also very helpful here. We want to make sure you satisfy visa requirements while also getting the full BYU MBA learning experience.
