We Care About You

Your success in this class is important to us; however, what we care about more than anything is your overall wellbeing. Let's take a look at some of the resources BYU and the Marriott School provide to help you manage your academic, mental, physical, social, and spiritual wellness. We invite you to consider pre-emptively using these resources at the beginning of the semester so you can become acquainted with them before life gets even more hectic. Remember, we as your instructors are also here to help. Please reach out to any of us at any time.  

If you know of any campus resources not included on this page that you have found beneficial to your own wellness, please let me know so we can include them!  

Academic Wellness

Academic Wellness

Academic Advisement by College Academic Support Office University Advisement Center
This link takes you to the respective advisement centers for each of the nine colleges on campus. They can help you plan your classes, graduate in a timely manner, and learn more about academic programs and careers.

 

 The Academic Support Office works to support student academic success, particularly degree-seeking, undergraduates who are struggling. The University Advisement Center provides academic advising and career counseling to students seeking information on how to choose majors or careers. The center serves as the college advisement center for all open-major students, but all students are welcome to use its services and resources for assistance in making informed decisions about majors and careers.
Learn Anywhere Multicultural Student Services University Accessibility Center
Struggling with knowing how to succeed in an online class? This website has tips, tricks, and other tools that can help you learn in a blended or virtual environment. Multicultural Student Services is a unique team of multicultural specialists who value the total development of the multicultural student within the aims of a BYU education. They seek to develop a BYU environment of “fellow citizenry” where multiculturalism can flourish.

 

 The University Accessibility Center seeks to provide students with disabilities equal access to all opportunities at BYU and to create an environment that facilitates learning and assists students in reaching their full potential.

Mental Wellness

Mental Wellness

BYU Marriott Counseling and Psychological Services Comprehensive Clinic Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)
The Marriott School provides a employs a licensed clinical social worker to provide prevention, wellness, and counseling services to its students. Students of any major who are enrolled in ACC 200 (and other business school classes) are eligible to use this service for free.

 

 The BYU Comprehensive clinic provides in-person and telemental health services for individuals, families, and couples.

 

 CAPS provides free group and individual counseling services for students. Walk-in appointments are available.

 
Sanvello SilverCloud Stress Management
Sanvello is the top app that offers on-demand support for stress, anxiety, and depression, providing clinically validated techniques to relieve your symptoms. This website contains information for how to download the app and use its services for free as a BYU student.

 

 SilverCloud is an online system designed to help you learn about and reduce symptoms of anxiety, depression, and stress. All full-time BYU students have free access to this resource. Make stress your friend! Learn about biofeedback, try some relaxation techniques, or make an appointment with an individual biofeedback specialist to learn how to manage stress.

Physical Wellness

Health Center Student Wellness YBeFit
The health center’s website lets you schedule an appointment with a doctor, make an appointment for a COVID-19 test or vaccine, or view the schedule for the urgent care clinic. This website provides a complete list of the fitness, physical health, nutritional health, and sports resources BYU offers to its students. Use this link to learn how to use the free on-campus fitness center, meet with a fitness trainer, or join an intramural sports team. This program centers around three important areas of disease prevention and health promotion: assessment of an individual’s present health and fitness, education/prescription to guide and motivate participants towards their wellness goals, and follow-up counseling and monitoring of progress to help individuals optimize outcomes.

Spiritual Wellness

Additional Resources

Additional Resources

Financial Help First-year Mentoring Honor Code Office
This website has links to learn about BYU Financial Aid, scholarships, student loans, tax help, and financial management and planning. Mentoring is designed to assist new students’ transitions into university life by pairing new students with trained upperclassmen. Every first-year student is assigned a peer mentor who will regularly reach out to you to help you succeed. Use this website to learn more about mentoring, connect with your mentor, or become a mentor yourself.

 

 The Honor Code Office aims to foster a community where students experience the fullness of their BYU education by practicing a commitment to excellence in behavior and by upholding the highest standards of academic and personal integrity at all times.
Title IX Office Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) Women's Services and Resources
The Title IX office provides resources for victims of dating violence, domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking–whether the offense occurred on or off campus. During tax season, experienced accounting students will do your taxes for you for free. This office is an information and referral network for students, staff, and faculty that addresses women’s issues in a positive, proactive way. You can visit their office to learn more about women’s issues, discuss family and career balance, or improve your nutrition habits. Their website also provides a list of other on-campus resources that women may find helpful.
